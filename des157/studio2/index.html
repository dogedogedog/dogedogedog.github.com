I don't like my handwriting and drawing style, so I collect all kinds of things (tickets, table paper, napkins, candy wrappers, etc.) and stick them into my scrapbook as my travel journal. I started this scrapbook since freshman, and now it is too full to close. When my friends come to visit me, I always share my traveling stories with them by looking at what I have collected inside my book.

Click on different images at the main page and see the story behind them.

I also have some empty pages in my scrapbook. The emptiness also means something.

I met with Eon-Joo in high school in North Carolina. I had never thought I could talk so much with a foreign friend. At the airport, she said, "we should meet each other at least once a year." And then we have never met again. :(

On July 2nd, 2018, I didn't bring all the documents to the visa officer at the Italian Embassy. I thought I could not start my quarter abroad program, but the officer wrote me what to do in details, explained everything to me extremely patiently, and waited me until I had collected everything. This was the beginning of my unforgettable journey.

Sydney and I were like a daughter and a mother in high school because I took a lot of care of her. She invited me to her favorite singer's concert, and that was the first time we met after graduating from high school.

Ceci is older and a little bit shorter than me, but she always wants to protect and help me because she thinks I'm so little LOL.

Last day of this quarter abroad program. All good things must come to an end.
